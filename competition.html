The diminishing-returns laws — applied to Westin
95-5 rule (Ehrenberg-Bass)
Only ~5% of HNI buyers are in-market at once; the long luxury-RE cycle means most content builds future mental availability. Keep always-on brand content — don't over-index on activation.
60 / 40 brand vs activation (Binet & Field)
For high-consideration RE, ~60% brand (amenities, Marriott-operated, reputation) / 40% activation (lead-gen, assured-rental, conquest). You're currently over-weighted on ads/activation.
ESOV — Extra Share of Voice (Binet & Field)
Your ad SOV (26 ads) trails leaders (Godrej 151). A modest SOV lift above your share-of-market drives growth, but efficiency flattens at very high SOV — out-target Godrej, don't out-shout them.
Ad-frequency fatigue
Meta frequency >3-5/user/week → CTR falls, CPA rises. Cap frequency and rotate creative. Since your reach is ~99% paid, this is your binding constraint.
Creative wear-out
Refresh ad/video creative every 2-4 weeks — the 3 promoted videos carrying 99% of your YouTube reach will fatigue and CPV will climb.
Posting-cadence saturation (organic)
Past ~3-5 posts/week per platform, per-post engagement drops (TARC 63/mo → 4 views; you post ~19/mo for ~323). Concentration beats volume.
Reach S-curve / channel saturation
Each channel saturates its addressable audience; incremental reach per ₹ falls. Diversify into testimonials, LinkedIn and Search rather than pouring more into a saturated feed.
